Privacy Concerns In The Modern World

Most of us would be having an Alexa device or security camera in our homes. You might think that all the data your camera sees safely gets dumped into your phone or your Alexa deletes everything it hears except for its wake word but no; that is not the case.

In this essay, we will discuss and see what companies do with our data with examples.

The Dark Secret

You are talking to your friend about a vacation to Singapore. There is also an Alexa device in the room, completely listening to the conversation. At the end, you finally decide the airline, accommodation, and travel spots. You play some songs with Alexa; you think that it has deaf ears until you call its name but no. While they claim to only record 'Wake Words,' thousands of 'false triggers' lead to private conversations being stored on company servers.

The "Data Brokerage"

We know Alexa, Siri, HomePod, or whatever listens or both listens and sees us but how would it use that data? They do a process named “Data Brokerage”. Let’s again use our example of two friends going to Singapore.

When Alexa hears their talks or their iPhones' Siri listens to it and stores it, they sell it. Whom do they sell it to? They sell the data to the airlines, hotel, and travel guides. Even I once noticed this when me and my father were talking about our vacation to Vietnam and after a few days, we saw a travel agent’s message.

The Ethical Question

Is this “Data Brokerage” ethical or unethical? In my opinion, it is definitely unethical. All citizens deserve their privacy. Companies might say that they only use it to train AI, but privacy should always come first. The hundred million people [1] who own a smart speaker deserve their privacy.

“Privacy is a fundamental human right.”

Tim Cook (CEO of Apple)
